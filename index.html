What I do

I am currently a junior at Cornell University studying Electrical and Computer Engineering with a minor in Computer Science. I am most interested in embedded systems, robotics, and software/hardware integration.

Northrop Grumman Corporation

During the Summer of 2018, I interned at the Northrop Grumman Corporation in Redondo Beach, CA. As an intern, I worked with metrology engineers and learned how to perform manual and automated calibrations for lab equipment such as oscilloscopes, power supplies, and digital multimeters. I also learned how to calibrate AC/DC standard references with metrology engineers in addition to performing virus scans, sanitization and imaging on DC/LF and Radio Frequency measurement and test equipment. At the end of my internship, I led a team of 4 interns to develop a poster and presentation on metrology and its role in the company for the Northrop Grumman Explore Aerospace Poster Competition.

CUSail // Cornell Autonomous Sailboat Team

I am the Business Subteam Lead for CUSail. CUSail is an interdisciplinary Cornell Engineering Project Team. We work together as a team to build a fully autonomous robotic sailboat for competition and oceanic research. I am in charge of the subteam that creates the team's budget, obtains corporate sponsors, documents team progress, and maintains the team presence on social media platorms. In the past, I was on the Mechanical Subteam. I was in charge of designing the deck and hull of the boat using CAD SolidWorks and eventually manufactuing the parts from composite materials such as fiber glass and carbon fiber.
